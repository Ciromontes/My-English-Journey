Job Interview Simulation

A simulated job interview in English showcasing communication skills

May 11, 2025

Practicing Job Interview Skills

In this video, I participate in a simulated job interview with Melissa to practice my English communication skills in a professional context.

Why Practice Job Interviews?

Job interviews in English can be challenging, even for those with good language skills. Practicing common interview questions helps build confidence and improves your ability to express yourself professionally.

The Interview Questions

During this simulated interview, I responded to several common job interview questions. Below is a breakdown of the questions and my responses:

My Response: "Hi, my name is Melissa. I'm 29 years old. I'm a professional in foreign trade, and currently, I'm studying software development."

Analysis: This is a brief introduction that covers my name, age, professional background, and current studies. In future interviews, I could expand on my relevant skills and experience.

My Response: "My weakness is that I sometimes feel a little shy about meetings, about talking with customers about what they need regarding software or tasks. That's a little weakness of mine. My strength is that I like to learn every day about new things and look for innovation in the process, and I like to solve problems."

Analysis: I identified a realistic weakness (shyness in meetings) and balanced it with strengths (continuous learning, innovation, problem-solving). This shows self-awareness and a growth mindset.

My Response: "Well, the opportunity that this job gives to me is that it is very near the development path that I want for my career, and I'm sure that working here, I will learn a lot of things. It's an opportunity to apply all my knowledge about software and gain experience to become a great senior developer."

Analysis: I focused on how the position aligns with my career goals and how I can grow professionally. This shows motivation and long-term thinking.

My Response: "When I was working in foreign trade, one of the hard things was that you have to make purchases from China and other countries, but you can't stay at the same time. They respond about 12 hours later, and you have to stay in communication even though you're not in the same place or working at the same time."

Analysis: I described a real challenge (time zone differences in international business) but could have elaborated more on specific strategies I used to overcome this challenge.

My Response: "Well, how I see my future in five years depends on which of my career plans. I want to finish my studies in software and become a software engineer, starting from a junior and becoming a senior developer. Another thing is that, as a professional in foreign trade, I want to apply the knowledge that I get in the career as a software engineer in solving problems related to moving commodities from one site to another."

Analysis: I outlined a clear career progression and showed how I plan to combine my background in foreign trade with my new skills in software development. This demonstrates ambition and strategic thinking.

Additional Questions

Question Key Points from My Response
What do you know about our company? I mentioned that it's a company focused on creating software applications to provide solutions for other companies in different areas.
How do you handle stress and pressure? I explained that I try to wait, count to 10, look at the problem from different perspectives, and ask for help from more experienced colleagues when needed.
Can you provide an example of when you worked as part of a team? I described my experience in foreign trade, working with different departments like marketing and manufacturing to coordinate product orders and deliveries.
Why did you leave your last job? I explained challenges in the foreign trade job market in Colombia, including high workload, long commutes, and compensation not matching responsibilities.
Do you have any questions for us? I asked about training opportunities and whether they could be completed remotely.

Reflection and Areas for Improvement

After reviewing this simulated interview, I've identified several areas where I can improve my English communication skills in professional contexts:

Pronunciation

Continue working on clear pronunciation, especially for technical terms related to software development and business.

Fluency

Practice speaking more smoothly without pauses or fillers like "um" and "well" at the beginning of responses.

Vocabulary

Expand my professional and industry-specific vocabulary to express ideas more precisely.

Confidence

Build confidence in speaking about my skills and achievements without downplaying them.

Conclusion

This simulated job interview was a valuable experience for practicing English in a professional context. By analyzing my responses and identifying areas for improvement, I can continue to develop my language skills for future career opportunities.
