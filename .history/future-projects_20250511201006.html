Planning My Future with Conditionals

In this presentation, I discuss my future projects and plans using what I've learned about conditional statements in English.

Professional Goals

Zero Conditional: General Truth

If I study English constantly, I improve my job opportunities.

This is a zero conditional because it expresses a general truth.

First Conditional: Real Possibility

If I complete my English studies, I will form remote job interviews in English.

This is a first conditional because it's a real possibility in the future.

If God permits, I will find a part-time job from Monday to Friday. This position allows me to balance my other activities.

Educational Goals

I'm currently studying software development at SENA. If I continue these studies, I will become a skilled programmer. This is another example of the first conditional.

I'm going to complete all my pending courses this year to expand my knowledge. One important project I'm working on is the veterinary clinic software. If I finish this project successfully, I will have a great addition to my portfolio.

First Conditional: Future Opportunity

If I study data analysis, I will enhance my technical profile and have more job opportunities in the future.

Spiritual Journey

My spiritual journey is also very important to me. If God guides me, I will participate in the Kingdom Evaluation School training that lasts for two months. If I complete this training successfully, I will have the opportunity to spread Bible teachings to remote areas.

Zero Conditional: Universal Truth

If people accept this message, they will find hope.

This expresses a universal truth. God is the true source of hope.

Entertainment Activities

In terms of entertainment activities, when I have free time, I will visit the zoo to see wild animals, especially Bengal tigers and other exotic species. If I visit the aquarium, I will observe many fish species through the glass. It is fascinating to watch marine life in their environment.

Zero Conditional: General Truth

If you appreciate nature, you understand the importance of conservation.

This is a zero conditional expressing a general truth about our relationship with the natural world.

Conclusion

To conclude my presentation, these are the main projects and plans I have for my future. Using conditionals helps express both general truths with the zero conditional and future possibilities with the first conditional.

Understanding Conditionals

Zero Conditional: Used for general truths or facts. Structure: If + present simple, present simple.

First Conditional: Used for real possibilities in the future. Structure: If + present simple, will + infinitive.

Using these structures correctly helps communicate future plans and possibilities clearly.
